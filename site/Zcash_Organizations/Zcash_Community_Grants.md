## Mission Statement

The Zcash Community Grants program funds independent teams entering the Zcash ecosystem, to perform major ongoing development (or other work) for the public good of the Zcash ecosystem. 

## People and Teams

The Zcash Community Grants is managed by a committee, which currently consists of five people. This community reviews and approves grant applications from third party teams who want to build on the Zcash protocol. More info on the [Team](https://zcashcommunitygrants.org/committee/)

Recent projects that have been approved by the ZCG are Zcash Shielded Assets (ZSA)  led by the QEDIT team. Bringing DeFi to ZEC with Zcash with a new payments protocol adding additional features to the Zcash mainnet. 

Zcash Media's Short Documentary featuring notable Zcashers such as Edward Snowden, Zooko & Deirdre Connolly with the purpose of creating relevant, entertaining and educational videos to be widely shared. 

Hardware wallets integration of Ledger to fully support Sapling shielded transactions, this allows Zcash to be stored privately & securely. This has been carried out by the Zondax Team. 

An Easy one-click shielded payment and Point-of-sale system for brick and mortar shops SDK being undertaken by ZGo. 

The Global Ambassador program helps Zcash gain broader representation internationally and for in-person community events around the world. 

Several more projects have been reviewed by the Zcash Grants Committee, see a [Full list](https://zcashgrants.org/gallery/25215916-53ea-4041-a3b2-6d00c487917d) of submitted projects.

## Organizational Structure

The Zcash Community Grants is an independent organization from the Electric Coin Company and the Zcash Foundation. 

## Funding

The Electric Coin Company receives 8% of the [Zcash development fund](https://zips.z.cash/zip-1014). They use these funds to support third party development teams and also for internal operations.

## How to Submit a Zcash Community Grant (ZCG) Application

Zcash Community Grants (ZCG) applications are submitted via GitHub Issues and discussed publicly on the Zcash Community Forum. Follow the steps below to submit a complete and valid application.

### 1. Prepare Your Proposal
Before submitting:
- Clearly define your project idea, goals, milestones, and deliverables.
- Prepare a budget (in USD) with justification.
- Ensure you have:
  - A GitHub account
  - A Zcash Community Forum account

### 2. Access the ZCG GitHub Repository
1. Log in to GitHub:  
   https://github.com
2. Navigate to the Zcash Community Grants repository:  
   https://github.com/ZcashCommunityGrants/zcashcommunitygrants
3. Review existing grant issues to understand the expected structure and level of detail.

### 3. Submit Your Grant Application via GitHub
1. Click the Issues tab in the repository.
2. Click New Issue.
3. Select the Grant Application template (if available).
4. Complete the application with the following information:
   - Project Title
   - Project Description & Objectives
   - Requested Funding Amount (USD)
   - Milestones & Deliverables
   - Timeline
   - Applicant / Team Information
   - Relevant Links or Supporting Materials
5. Review your submission for clarity and completeness.
6. Click Submit new issue.

This GitHub issue serves as your official grant application.

### 4. Post Your Application on the Zcash Community Forum
After submitting your GitHub issue:

1. Copy the URL of your GitHub grant issue.
2. Visit the Zcash Community Forum:  
   https://forum.zcashcommunity.com
3. Navigate to Community Grants â†’ Applications.
4. Create a new topic that includes:
   - A short summary of your proposal
   - A link to your GitHub grant application
   - An invitation for community feedback
5. Publish the forum post.

Note:  
If you are unable to create a new topic due to forum trust-level restrictions, leave a comment on your GitHub issue requesting assistance from a moderator.

### 5. Community Feedback Period
- Allow enough time for community discussion and feedback.
- Respond constructively to questions or suggestions on the forum.

### 6. ZCG Committee Review
- The ZCG committee reviews:
  - Your GitHub grant application
  - Feedback from the community forum
- You may be asked to clarify or revise parts of your proposal.
- Funding decisions are made transparently.

### 7. Approval, Milestones, and Reporting
If your application is approved:
1. Complete any required onboarding or documentation.
2. Deliver work according to the approved milestone schedule.
3. Post regular progress updates on your forum thread.
4. Request milestone payments after deliverables are completed and approved.
