## Shielded Labs / ZODL / Zcash Foundation Updates

### 1. Orchard Soundness Vulnerability Discovered and Remediated
**Project:** Shielded Labs / ZODL / Zcash Foundation
**Date:** May 29 – June 3, 2026 (public disclosure June 4–5, 2026)

Security researcher Taylor Hornby, contracted by Shielded Labs, discovered a critical soundness flaw in the Orchard shielded pool's zero-knowledge proof circuit. The bug, present since Orchard's launch in May 2022, could have allowed unlimited, undetectable counterfeit ZEC creation within the shielded pool. Shielded Labs coordinated a two-phase emergency response: a soft fork (Zebra 4.5.3) on June 2 temporarily disabled Orchard transactions, followed by the NU6.2 hard fork on June 3 (block 3,364,600) that corrected the circuit and re-enabled Orchard. No exploitation was confirmed, and Zcash's turnstile accounting mechanism verified no unauthorized supply inflation.

**Sources:**
- https://zodl.com/orchard-vulnerability-successfully-remediated/
- https://zodl.com/ironwood-a-new-shielded-pool-for-zcash/
- https://forum.zcashcommunity.com/t/four-questions-about-the-orchard-vulnerability/56249
- https://blocksec.com/blog/web3-security-zcash-orchard-soundness-bug-analysis

**Why it matters:** The most significant security event in Zcash's recent history. It demonstrates both the risks of complex ZK circuits and the ecosystem's capacity for rapid, coordinated emergency fixes — critical for community confidence.

### 2. Ironwood: A New Shielded Pool Proposed
**Project:** Shielded Labs / ZODL
**Date:** June 6–17, 2026

Following the Orchard incident, Shielded Labs and ZODL jointly proposed Ironwood — a new shielded pool with turnstile accounting designed to enable verifiable supply integrity without compromising user privacy. The proposal targets a late-July activation and has been posted for community review.

**Sources:**
- https://zodl.com/ironwood-a-new-shielded-pool-for-zcash/

**Why it matters:** Ironwood represents a potential paradigm shift in how Zcash balances privacy guarantees with supply auditability, potentially producing new cryptographic primitives that become industry standards.

### 3. Zebra 4.5.3 and 5.0.0 — Emergency Soft Fork and NU6.2 Activation
**Project:** Zcash Foundation
**Date:** June 3, 2026

The Foundation released Zebra 4.5.3 and 5.0.0 to address the Orchard issue, implementing emergency measures and activating NU6.2. The majority of network hash rate now runs Zebra rather than zcashd, confirming a key decentralization milestone. No chain split occurred during the transition.

**Source:**
- https://zfnd.org/zebra-4-5-3-and-5-0-0-emergency-soft-fork-and-nu6-2-activation/

**Why it matters:** Strengthens node diversity and network resilience through the memory-safe Rust implementation, while confirming Zebra as the primary consensus client.
